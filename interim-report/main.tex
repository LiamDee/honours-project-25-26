Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) is a psychological framework that take an "active, problem-focused, 
time-sensitive treatment approach" aiming to increase adaptive 
behaviour and reduce emotional distress in an individual with mental health issues via targeted, short-term 
strategies that intends to alter maladaptive emotional responses in an individual by changing their thoughts and/or

standard" \autocite{RefWorks:RefID:141-beck2020cognitive} due to the proven success the framework has had on 
treating certain issues such as nicotine overdependence, schizophrenia, 
depression, and anxiety \autocite{RefWorks:RefID:140-hofmann2012efficacy}. \newline

a patient in positive activities and helping with aversive control 
\autocite{RefWorks:RefID:146-tindall2017behavioural}), cognitive restructuring (A process that assists patients

previous study, showing that developing for Android allowed for additional functionality not possible with iOS.\newline

\subsection{Research Method}
This project aims to create a smartphone \& social media addiction mobile intervention app that utilises 
psychological frameworks and usage tracking and find if it reduces smartphone \& SM usage in young adults 
more than traditional intervention apps that use app-restriction features. The project will follow a develop and 
test methodology. \newline

A mobile application that integrates a psychological framework alongside usage tracking functionalities will
be created, then at the conclusion of development, testing and evaluating the effectiveness of the app will begin, which will 
involve comparing the results of using the developed app for three weeks with an existing app that uses 
app-restriction functionalities for the same time period with participants. This testing method has been used by 
other researchers who have conducted research into the topic 
\autocite{RefWorks:RefID:147-throuvala2020mind,RefWorks:RefID:42-esmaeili2018method}. The results of this will then
be evaluated to find if it satisfies the problem statement and hypothesis.


\subsection {Develop the psychology-based intervention mobile application}
\subsubsection {Development}
% android, java, android studio ide, because android is open source
From the literature and technology review, the platform that the app will be
developed for will be Android. This is because Android applications easily allow for requesting usage 
statistics of apps installed on a user's phone via the android.app.usage package in the Android API, while Apple's
iOS operating system doesn't allow for this due to privacy concerns. The app will be developed using the Java
programming language, and use Android Studio as its IDE, as it's the IDE recommended by Google for developing
Android apps. \newline

% is an open source operating system, meaning that

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy and Reality Therapy were both discussed as psychological frameworks that could be
used, and from the research carried out, Reality Therapy was decided on as the framework to integrate.
This is because one of the main criticisms of SA \& SMA intervention apps was the lack of opportunity to learn
about SA \& SMA, and Reality Therapy's aim is to make a patient aware if their actions get in their way of their
goals, and then replace that action with a healthier alternative. Meanwhile, Cognitive Behavioural Therapy's
methods don't innately provide teaching opportunities in its framework. 

\subsubsection {Project Lifecycle}
% develop and test? agile? iterative?
This project will make use
of an agile methodology during development. The Agile methodology is a framework that allows for iteration
and flexibility throughout the software development lifecycle in response to user and stakeholder demands, an
advantage over traditional methodologies such as the waterfall methodology 
\autocite{RefWorks:RefID:149-al-saqqa2020agile, RefWorks:RefID:148-altameem2015impact}.
 
\subsubsection{Testing}
Testing will consist of integration testing and system testing. Integration testing will occur once
development reaches certain milestones, such as when implementation of a component is
completed, where it will then be tested against a set of test cases. Development will then progress to the next 
milestone once at a satisfactory level. Near the end of development, the prototype
will undergo system testing, where the entire prototype is tested from the perspective of an end-user for 
functional and non-functional elements \autocite{RefWorks:RefID:150-loikkanen2024improving}.

\subsection{Evaluation}
% see section 1.2.1 for more info
%\subsubsection{Usability Testing}
%\subsubsection{Evaluation}

Evaluating the application will involve gathering 10 participants to partake in testing the app. The evaluation will 
involve splitting participants into two equal-sized groups, one of which will use the
developed app, while the other will use a separate app that only provides app restriction features to evaluate the 
app's effectiveness. After a three week testing period, participants will be asked to provide statistics about their 
app usage over the three weeks. Those in the group using the developed app will also receive a survey regarding their 
experience with the app. The results from this testing period will then be used to evaluate the app's effectiveness 
and identify any areas of further work.
